# CD Maturity Cold Email Sequence

**Target:** CD holders whose CDs are maturing or recently matured
**Goal:** Get a reply or phone appointment
**Cadence:** Day 0 → Day 4-5 → Day 12-13

## Email 2: The Tax Angle
**Subject:** `the 1099 question`
**Send:** Day 4-5

Hi {{FirstName}},

One thing that catches CD holders off guard every February — the 1099-INT. You're paying taxes on your CD interest every single year, even if you never touch the money.

With a fixed annuity, that same growth is tax-deferred. You don't owe anything until you withdraw. Over 3–5 years on a $100K deposit, that difference adds up to thousands in taxes you didn't have to pay yet.

And if you're in Florida, there's another angle worth knowing — a properly structured annuity can protect those assets under Medicaid rules. Unlike gifting money (which triggers a 5-year look-back penalty), an annuity isn't treated as a gift.

Happy to run a quick side-by-side comparison with your actual CD numbers. Takes about 10 minutes.

Worth a look?

Steve

## Personalization Tips

- If you know their **CD maturity date**, drop it into Email 1: "I noticed your CD matures on {{date}} — you've got a narrow window before it auto-renews"
- If you know the **CD amount**, use it in Email 2: "On a ${{amount}} deposit, that difference adds up to..."
- If you know their **community/area**, use it in Email 3 for social proof
- Send **plain text** — no HTML, images, or multiple links
- Best send times: **Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 AM**
- Track **replies**, not opens

## Key Talking Points (from Duet CRM)

- Tax-deferred growth (no annual 1099)
- 10% annual penalty-free withdrawal
- Principal protection — no market risk
- Guaranteed minimum rate (CDs reset at maturity, annuities lock in a floor)
- FL Medicaid asset protection (not treated as a gift)
- Probate avoidance — goes directly to beneficiaries
- Surrender charges decrease yearly and disappear after 3-5 years (CDs have early withdrawal penalties too)
