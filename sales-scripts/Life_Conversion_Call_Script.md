# Life Conversion Call Script
## 5-Stage Framework for Converting Renewable Term to Fixed Term / Universal Life

---

## Stage 1: The Connection (The Disarm)
**Goal:** Address the direct mail piece, lower their guard, set the agenda.

### Checkpoints:
1. Reference the returned card / rate increase notice
2. Identify yourself as their assigned account lead
3. Ask: stop rate hikes or just look at options?
4. Lower status — "I don't have the final say, just seeing what you're eligible for"
5. Ask permission to ask questions about current policy

### Key Phrases:
- "returned a card to us"
- "upcoming rate increases"
- "assigned account lead"
- "stop those rate hikes"
- "look over your options"
- "I don't have the final say"
- "eligible for based on your age and health"
- "you might be better off staying with what you have"

### Tone: Calm, slightly confused, non-threatening

---

## Stage 2: The Engagement (85% of the Sale)
**Goal:** Expose the 5-year renewable trap and make them feel the pain of future rate hikes.

### 2A: Situation (Fact Finding)
6. What do you have in place right now?
7. How long have you had the policy?
8. Are you on your own or have family?
9. What caused you to go with the 5-year renewable?

### 2B: Problem Awareness (Finding the Pain)
10. Do you like that they're hiking your rates?
11. Clarify: "how do you mean exactly?"
12. Every 5 years they force a higher rate — how much longer do you want to keep paying?

### 2C: Solution Awareness (The Ideal Future)
13. Had you been looking for strategies to lock in your rate?
14. If we could get you approved for a 100% locked-in rate — what would that do for you?

### 2D: Consequence (Building Urgency)
15. What happens if price jumps so high you drop it, then something happens?
16. Do you want to put her in that situation if you didn't have to?

### Key Phrases:
- "what exactly do you have in place"
- "how long have you had that"
- "what caused you to go with"
- "hike up your rates"
- "how do you mean exactly"
- "renewable terms every 5 years"
- "force you to pay a higher rate"
- "completely out of hand"
- "lock in your rate permanently"
- "100% locked in"
- "never force you to pay a rate hike again"
- "what would that do for you"
- "can't afford it so you have to drop it"
- "what would they do to pay the mortgage"
- "do you want to put her in that situation"

### Tone: Empathetic, concerned, probing — never aggressive

---

## Stage 3: The Transition (The Bridge)
**Goal:** Link rate hike pain to your solutions.

### Checkpoints:
17. Summarize back: want family protected + renewable keeps jacking rates
18. Acknowledge the stress about affording it
19. "I have a couple of options that would fit exactly"
20. "Do you have a pen handy?"

### Key Phrases:
- "based on what you've told me"
- "what we do would actually work for you"
- "you said you want to make sure"
- "keeps jacking up the rates"
- "making you feel a bit stressed"
- "couple of options"
- "fit exactly what you're looking for"
- "pen handy"

### Tone: Confident, solution-oriented

---

## Stage 4: The Presentation (The Cure)
**Goal:** Present Fixed Term and Universal Life as cures to the 5-year trap.

### Checkpoints:
21. Name the problem: "5-year renewable traps"
22. Option 1: Fixed Term — locked rate for 15 or 20 years
23. Option 2: Universal Life — locked in for life, never re-qualify
24. Ask which makes more sense: locking in for 20 years or forever?

### Key Phrases:
- "5-year renewable traps"
- "Fixed Term policy"
- "lock your rate in"
- "15 or 20 years"
- "price stays the exact same"
- "can never raise it on you"
- "Universal Life"
- "lasts forever"
- "never have to re-qualify medically"
- "locked in for the rest of your life"
- "does that make sense"
- "which one do you feel makes the most sense"

### Tone: Clear, educational — two options, their choice

---

## Stage 5: The Commitment (The Close)
**Goal:** Get them to persuade themselves to apply today.

### Checkpoints:
25. "Why do you feel like it is, though?" (they defend their choice)
26. First step: get medically approved
27. Start collecting info — move into application

### Key Phrases:
- "why do you feel like it is though"
- "the first step"
- "medically approved"
- "get that pulled up right here"
- "first name spelled"
- "is that correct"
- "middle initial"

### Tone: Calm, assumptive — application is the natural next step

---

## Curveball Scenarios

### Tangents:
- Client starts complaining about their current insurance company
- Client asks about bundling with auto/home insurance
- Client brings up what they saw on a TV commercial
- Client wants to know about whole life / cash value

### Emotional Reactions:
- Client gets angry about rate increases
- Client worries about medical exam / health disqualification
- Client says "I knew insurance was a scam"
- Spouse disagrees in background

### Off-Topic Objections:
- "Can I just cancel and go without insurance?"
- "My work gives me life insurance, maybe I'll just use that"
- "What if I just switch to another 5-year term somewhere cheaper?"
- "I don't want to do a medical exam"
- "Let me shop around first and compare"
