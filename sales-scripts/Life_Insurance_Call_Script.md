# Life Insurance / Final Expense Call Script
## 5-Stage Framework for In-Branch Life Conversations

---

## Stage 1: The Connection (The Disarm)
**Goal:** Address the AAA context, remove sales pressure, set the agenda.

### Checkpoints:
1. AAA context opener — most people think AAA is just towing/trip-tiks
2. Confirm they came in for final expense options
3. Lower status — "I don't have the final say, my job is to see what you're eligible for"
4. Ask: basic funeral/cremation or leave extra behind for kids/grandkids?

### Key Phrases:
- "a lot of people think AAA just does the trip-tiks and towing"
- "final expense options we have for our members"
- "I don't have the final say"
- "see what you might even be eligible for"
- "basic funeral and cremation expenses"
- "leave a little bit extra behind"

### Tone: Warm, casual, non-threatening

---

## Stage 2: The Engagement (Building Urgency & Pain)
**Goal:** Get them to visualize the burden falling on their children.

### 2A: Situation (Reality Check)
5. Confirm no current life insurance
6. Ask what they have in place (savings/assets) to cover funeral and medical bills

### 2B: Problem Awareness (Funeral Director Probe)
7. Who in their family would have to sit down with the funeral director?
8. Funeral directors play off emotions — expensive casket/service
9. Would their child have to scrounge money or get a loan with interest?

### 2C: Solution Awareness (The Relief)
10. If we got you approved today — knowing kids wouldn't stress about money while grieving — what would that do for you?

### 2D: Consequence (In-Office Urgency)
11. What happens if you walk out today without coverage and pass away sooner than expected?
12. Do you want them to go through that if they didn't have to?

### Key Phrases:
- "if something were to happen to you unexpectedly"
- "what do you currently have in place"
- "who would be the person responsible"
- "sit down and actually meet the funeral director"
- "funeral directors often play off emotions"
- "scrounge the money together"
- "go out and get a loan"
- "wouldn't have to stress about coming up with the money while they're grieving"
- "what would that do for you personally"
- "walk out of the office today without any coverage"
- "passing away weeks or months before you thought you would"
- "do you want them to have to go through all of that"

### Tone: Empathetic, concerned, slowed-down — never aggressive

---

## Stage 3: The Transition (The Bridge)
**Goal:** Link the emotional pain of burdening kids to the solution.

### Checkpoints:
13. Summarize back: they want kids protected + no coverage in place
14. Acknowledge the worry/burden they feel
15. "I have an option that would fit exactly what you're looking for"

### Key Phrases:
- "based on what you've told me"
- "what we do for our members would actually work for you"
- "you said you want to make sure your kids don't have to pay"
- "don't have any coverage in place"
- "making you feel a little worried about leaving them with a burden"
- "I have an option here that would fit exactly"

### Tone: Confident, solution-oriented

---

## Stage 4: The Presentation (The Cure)
**Goal:** Keep it simple. High/Medium/Low options. No jargon.

### Checkpoints:
16. Name it: state-regulated Final Expense policy
17. Key differentiator: cost is locked in, no rate hikes as you age
18. Check for agreement: "Does that make sense?"
19. Present 3 options (High/Medium/Low) with monthly costs
20. Guide to middle option — covers funeral + little extra for inflation

### Key Phrases:
- "state-regulated Final Expense policy"
- "cost is locked in"
- "stays consistent"
- "never have to worry about rate hikes"
- "does that make sense"
- "three options"
- "most of our clients your age go with the middle option"
- "covers the funeral completely"
- "leaves a little extra for inflation"
- "which one would you lean towards"

### Tone: Simple, educational — teaching not selling

---

## Stage 5: The Commitment (The Close)
**Goal:** Lock them in and start paperwork immediately.

### Checkpoints:
21. "Do you feel like this could be the answer to take that burden off your kids?"
22. CRUCIAL: "Why do you feel it is, though?" (they vocalize why they need it)
23. Assume the application: "first step is to see if we can get you medically approved"
24. Start collecting info: "I've got your first name spelled... is that correct?"

### Key Phrases:
- "take that burden off your kids"
- "why do you feel it is though"
- "the very first step"
- "see if we can even get you medically approved"
- "pulled up right here on my computer"
- "is that correct"
- "middle initial"

### Tone: Calm, assumptive — paperwork is the natural next step

---

## Curveball Scenarios

### Tangents:
- Client talks about their health issues at length
- Client brings up their late spouse
- Client asks about AAA membership benefits/discounts
- Client wants to compare with AARP or colonial penn ads

### Emotional Reactions:
- Client gets emotional thinking about dying
- Client says "I don't want to think about this"
- Client gets defensive: "Are you saying I'm going to die soon?"
- Client's adult child calls mid-conversation

### Off-Topic Objections:
- "My kids can figure it out, they have good jobs"
- "I saw those Tom Selleck commercials, is this the same thing?"
- "Can't I just pre-pay the funeral home directly?"
- "What if I outlive the policy?"
- "My church will take care of everything"
