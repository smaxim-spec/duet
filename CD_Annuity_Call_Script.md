# CD-to-Annuity Conversion Call Script
## 5-Stage Framework for In-Branch CD Conversations

---

## Stage 1: The Connection
**Goal:** Disarm the prospect, neutralize expectations, get them to open up.

### Checkpoints:
1. Acknowledge why they came in (CD rates)
2. Ask: short-term park or longer-term strategy?
3. Introduce "hidden gaps" concept (taxes, inflation)
4. Ask permission to ask questions

### Key Phrases:
- "before we just set that up for you"
- "were you looking to just park this money, or a longer-term strategy"
- "hidden gaps in how you're shielding this money from taxes and inflation"
- "would it be appropriate if I asked a few questions"

### Tone: Curious, neutral, non-threatening

---

## Stage 2: The Engagement (85% of the sale)
**Goal:** Uncover real situation, build the gap, make them feel the pain of CD limitations.

### 2A: Situation Questions
4. Walk me through your current retirement strategy
5. What caused you to want a CD for these funds?

### 2B: Problem Awareness
6. Is there anything you would change about the CD?
7. Clarify the tax pain ("how do you mean exactly?")
8. Probe the impact ("has that had an impact on your timeline?")
9. Challenge: returns after factoring in inflation?
10. Key question: why another CD vs. a vehicle with same safety but tax protection?

### 2C: Solution Awareness
11. Have you been looking at other strategies?
12. What's prevented you from looking previously?
13. Paint the picture: "if we could put you in a position where your principal was 100% protected, but compounded tax-deferred..."

### 2D: Consequence
14. Challenge: what happens if you keep rolling CDs for 5-10 years and inflation outpaces returns?
15. "Are you willing to settle for that, though... if you didn't have to?"

### Key Phrases:
- "walk me through"
- "what caused you to"
- "how do you mean exactly"
- "has that had an impact"
- "just so I can see the rationale"
- "what's prevented you"
- "100% protected from market losses"
- "compounded tax-deferred"
- "what happens if you don't do anything"
- "are you willing to settle for that"

### Tone: Empathetic, concerned, genuinely curious — never pushy

---

## Stage 3: The Transition
**Goal:** Bridge smoothly from admitted problems to solution.

### Checkpoints:
16. Summarize back what they told you (safety need + tax/inflation pain)
17. Confirm it's important to act
18. "I have an option that would fit exactly what you're looking for"

### Key Phrases:
- "based on what you've told me"
- "you said you absolutely need this money to be safe"
- "sticking it in a CD is causing you to lose money to taxes and inflation"
- "I have an option here that would fit exactly"

### Tone: Confident, solution-oriented

---

## Stage 4: The Presentation
**Goal:** Present annuity ONLY as cure to their specific problems. No generic pitch.

### Checkpoints:
19. Name the product (Fixed/Indexed Annuity)
20. Connect to their #1 need: principal protection (same as CD)
21. Differentiate: tax-deferred growth
22. Translate to their life: "retire on time"
23. Check for agreement: "Does that make sense?"

### Key Phrases:
- "principal is 100% locked in and protected"
- "grows tax-deferred"
- "aren't paying taxes on the growth year after year"
- "compound much faster"
- "eliminate the tax drag and inflation risk"
- "does that make sense"
- "do you see how that could help you"

### Tone: Clear, educational — teaching not selling

---

## Stage 5: The Commitment
**Goal:** Get them to logically persuade themselves to take the next step.

### Checkpoints:
24. "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you?"
25. CRUCIAL: "Why do you feel it is, though?" (they defend the annuity)
26. Next steps: allocate funds + start paperwork
27. "Would that be appropriate, or how would you like to proceed?"

### Key Phrases:
- "do you feel like this could be the answer"
- "why do you feel it is though"
- "we've covered the bases"
- "how much of these funds you want to allocate"
- "stop losing that money to taxes"
- "would that be appropriate"
- "how would you like to proceed"

### Tone: Calm, assumptive — let them close themselves

---

## Curveball Scenarios (things that pull you off script)

### Tangents:
- Client starts talking about their grandkids / vacation / health
- Client asks about stock market / Bitcoin / real estate
- Client brings up what their neighbor told them
- Client asks about rates at other banks
- Client wants to talk about their existing advisor

### Emotional Reactions:
- Client gets defensive: "Are you trying to sell me something?"
- Client gets overwhelmed: "This is a lot of information"
- Client gets suspicious: "What's in it for you?"
- Client spouse disagrees mid-conversation
- Client brings up a bad past experience with insurance

### Off-Topic Objections:
- "My son told me annuities are bad"
- "I saw something on the news about insurance companies"
- "Can I just do a money market instead?"
- "What about I-Bonds?"
- "I need to talk to my accountant first"

### Recovery Target:
- Acknowledge the tangent (1 exchange)
- Bridge back to where you were in the script (1 exchange)
- Resume the next checkpoint (1 exchange)
- Best case: 3 exchanges or fewer to recover
