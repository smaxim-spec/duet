# Long-Term Care Call Script
## 5-Stage Framework for In-Office LTC / Eclipse Protector Conversations

---

## Stage 1: The Connection (The In-Office Disarm)
**Goal:** Lower guard, establish expert status, find out why they came in.

### Checkpoints:
1. Warm greeting — acknowledge why they came in (walk-in or referral)
2. Ask what brought them in today
3. Lower status — "I don't have the final say, just seeing eligibility"
4. Ask permission to ask questions about what they have in place

### Key Phrases:
- "what brought you into the office today"
- "my front desk mentioned"
- "I don't have the final say"
- "see what you would even be eligible for"
- "based on your age and health"
- "would you be opposed"
- "if we could even help you in the first place"

### Tone: Warm, relaxed, non-threatening

---

## Stage 2: The Engagement (Building the Gap)
**Goal:** They don't have cash reserves to self-insure. Doing nothing burdens their family.

### 2A: Situation (Fact Finding)
5. If something happened tomorrow — what do you have in place for nursing/in-home care?
6. Medicare only covers short-term rehab — do you have $150K-$200K set aside?

### 2B: Problem Awareness (Finding the Pain)
7. Who would be responsible for caring for you or paying for a facility?
8. What would that do to them — quit job? Scrounge money? Get a loan?
9. Identity frame: "some people don't mind putting that stress on their kids"

### 2C: Solution Awareness (The Ideal Future)
10. Before today, had you been looking for strategies to protect your family?
11. If approved for $4,100+/month — can even pay family to be caregiver — what would that do for you?

### 2D: Consequence (In-Office Urgency)
12. What happens if you walk out today, keep putting it off, and have a major health event?
13. What will your family do when they have to drain their savings?
14. Do you want to put them in that situation if you didn't have to?

### Key Phrases:
- "walk me through what you currently have in place"
- "Medicare usually only covers short-term rehabilitation"
- "$150,000 to $200,000 sitting in a bank account"
- "who would be the person responsible"
- "quit her job to take care of you"
- "scrounge the money together"
- "burden"
- "some people don't mind putting that stress on their kids"
- "$4,100 every single month"
- "pay them to take care of you at home"
- "what happens if you walk out today"
- "drain their own savings"
- "do you want to put them in that situation"

### Tone: Concerned, empathetic, identity-framing

---

## Stage 3: The Transition (The Bridge)
**Goal:** Link emotional pain of burdening family to the Securian illustration.

### Checkpoints:
15. Summarize: don't want to be a burden + no plan in place
16. Acknowledge the worry about family's future
17. "I have an option that would fit exactly what you're looking for"
18. "Do you have a pen handy?"

### Key Phrases:
- "based on what you've told me"
- "what we do would actually work for you"
- "you said you absolutely do not want to be a burden"
- "don't have a plan or the cash reserves"
- "making you feel worried"
- "fit exactly what you're looking for"
- "pen handy"

### Tone: Confident, solution-oriented

---

## Stage 4: The Presentation (The Cure)
**Goal:** Present Eclipse Protector II as the cure. Emphasize monthly benefit and freedom of use.

### Checkpoints:
19. Name the product: Eclipse Protector Indexed Universal Life
20. Explain the Long-Term Care Agreement
21. Death benefit: $200K tax-free
22. Living benefits: $4,167/month for 4 years if chronically ill
23. Cash indemnity — freedom to spend however (facility, home care, pay family)
24. Premium: $629/month with No Lapse Guarantee
25. Check: "Do you see how that could help protect your family?"

### Key Phrases:
- "Eclipse Protector"
- "Long-Term Care Agreement"
- "death benefit"
- "tax-free"
- "living benefits"
- "$4,167 every single month"
- "4 years"
- "chronically ill"
- "cash indemnity"
- "freedom"
- "pay for a nursing facility"
- "pay them to be your caregiver"
- "No Lapse Guarantee"
- "locked in and guaranteed"
- "does that make sense"
- "do you see how that could help"

### Tone: Clear, educational — numbers-driven, not jargon

---

## Stage 5: The Commitment (The Close)
**Goal:** They logically persuade themselves the premium is worth removing the burden.

### Checkpoints:
26. "Do you feel like this could be the answer?"
27. "Why do you feel like it is though?" (they defend the purchase)
28. Next steps: application + health questionnaire for medical approval
29. Start collecting info — move into application

### Key Phrases:
- "do you feel like this could be the answer"
- "why do you feel like it is though"
- "covered the bases"
- "submit an application"
- "health questionnaire"
- "medically approved"
- "pulled up right here"
- "first name spelled"
- "is that correct"

### Tone: Calm, assumptive — application is the natural next step
