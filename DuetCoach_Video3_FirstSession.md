# Video 3: Your First Practice Session
## Target length: 5-6 minutes

---

## Loom Setup
- Record desktop, your face in corner
- Be signed into DuetCoach with both API keys configured
- Be on the Practice tab to start
- Set Voice Mode toggle ON

---

## Script

### [0:00] Intro
"Hey, this is Steve. Now that your API keys are set up, let me walk you through your first practice session. I'll show you the three modes, what to expect, and what to focus on as a beginner."

### [0:20] The Three Modes
"At the top of the Practice tab you'll see three big cards:

**Scripted mode** is free. The conversations are pre-built — the AI matches keywords in what you say to pick its responses. Good for learning the flow without burning API credit.

**Claude AI mode** uses your Anthropic key. The client responds dynamically — like a real conversation. This is what feels most realistic.

**Script Training mode** is my favorite. It uses both APIs. The client talks back like in Claude AI mode, but it also throws curveballs at you — random distractions, off-topic questions, emotional reactions — and scores you on how fast you get back on script.

For your first session, let's just use **Claude AI mode**. Click that one."

[Show: the 3 mode cards, clicking Claude AI]

### [1:00] Picking a Persona
"Below the mode cards, you'll see categories: CD-to-Annuity, Life Insurance, Life Conversion, Long-Term Care, Kids IUL, and IUL.

Each category has 3 personas with different difficulty levels — Easy, Medium, and Hard. I'd recommend starting with an **Easy** persona in whatever category you sell most.

Let's start with **Margaret Chen** — she's the easiest CD-to-Annuity persona. She's a retired widow with $150K in CDs maturing soon. She's open to options."

[Show: clicking Margaret Chen]

### [1:30] The Chat Interface
"Margaret will start the conversation. You'll hear her voice if voice mode is on. Notice the **REC indicator** in the top right — that means the session is being recorded.

To respond, you have two options:
- **Type** in the text box at the bottom and click Send
- **Press and hold the mic button** like a walkie-talkie. Speak. Release to send.

Voice mode is the most realistic, but typing works fine too — especially when you're learning the script."

[Show: Margaret's first message, demonstrate the mic button briefly]

### [2:00] Walking Through a Conversation
"Let me do a quick exchange. Margaret says she has CDs and is looking at her options. Notice the **5 phase tracker** at the top — those are the framework stages: Situation, Problem, Solution, Consequence, Commitment.

The goal is to walk through these in order. Don't jump straight to pitching the annuity — that's a big mistake."

[Show: speaking your first response, asking a situation question. Show phase tracker advancing.]

### [3:00] Show 2-3 More Exchanges
[Continue the conversation, demonstrating:]
- Asking open-ended questions
- Letting Margaret talk
- Acknowledging her concerns

"You can take as long as you want. There's no time limit. The goal is to practice the conversation flow, not race through it."

### [3:45] Ending the Session
"When you're done — either you've closed her or you want to wrap up — click the red **'End'** button.

A score modal pops up with your 5-category breakdown. Don't worry about a low score — first sessions usually run 40-60. The goal is to see where you're weak and improve from there."

[Show: clicking End, score appearing]

### [4:15] The Notes Field — Most Important Thing
"Here's the part most people skip. **Write notes after every session.**

What felt off? Where did you struggle? What did you learn? Even one sentence is enough.

Over time, when you look back at your notes, you'll spot patterns. 'I always rush past Problem Awareness.' 'I forget the identity frame question.' That's where real improvement happens.

Click **Save & New Scenario** to save your notes."

[Show: typing a note, clicking Save]

### [4:45] Stats and History
"Click the **History** tab to see all your past sessions. You can listen back to recordings (if you had voice mode on), see your score breakdown, and edit your notes anytime.

Click **Stats** to see your trends — what's your weakest scoring dimension? What's your streak? That's how you know what to work on."

[Show: History tab, expand a session, then Stats tab]

### [5:15] Daily Habit Tip
"My recommendation: do **one session a day, 10-15 minutes**. That's it. The agents who practice daily outsell the ones who don't. That's not marketing — it's just true.

Pick a time that works for you. For me it's right after my morning coffee, before I make any real calls. Builds the muscle.

That's it — you're set. Hit the feedback button if anything breaks or feels off. Talk soon."
